Germany Fieldwork

In December 2024, two of our team members, Erika and Lanka, receieved funding through the Common Mission Project to visit the Joint Multinational Readiness Center in Hohenfels, Germany. The base was the source of the problem statement our team tackled during the Innovating for National Security program at UC San Diego.

Our initial MVP revolved around using the Tactical Voice Bridge (TVB) to connect the legacy SINCGAR radios used by Observer Coach Trainer (OCT) Teams, and the modern Integrated Tactical Network (ITN) radios used by training forces. While our research into the device's capabilities indicated the connection should work, we needed to verify this before moving forward. Our Innovating for National Security (i4NS) Team Sponsor, Captain Christian Perez from the JMRC procured a TVB and both radio types, allowing our team members to physically verify that the device can connect the formerly incompatible equipment.

Through numerous interviews with OCTs and other JMRC training staff, Erika and Lanka uncovered a key overarching challenge the base faced beyond incompatible equipment: the decentralization of training data. While there were existing systems in place to attempt to monitor geolocation and audio data during training sessions, they were largely ineffective and not consolidated in an accessible format. The staff unanimously agreed that the incompatibility of equipment coupled with a lack of access to data-driven insights severely hinders training effectiveness. Seeing how crucial overcoming this challenge would be, our team was even more assured that the data centralization dashboard MVP we designed would be invaluable.

Erika and Lanka presented our monitoring dashboard prototypes to JMRC staff, collecting key critiques for further iteration. The feedback received was overwhelmingly positive, validating our proposed solution for providing OCTs with the ability to monitor all aspects of training sessions. OCTs emphasized the need for real-time audio transcripts, visualized geolocation data and event tagging to bring up relevant data during After Action Reviews. With this confirmation that our vision of a centralized hub of data streams is crucial to addressing their major pain points, we have moved forward with development.
